# 🌍 International Education Cost Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes the **cost of studying abroad across different countries, universities, and program levels**.  

The goal is to help prospective students make **data-driven financial decisions** by identifying:
- Affordable study destinations  
- Cost drivers (tuition, rent, etc.)  
- Impact of program duration on total cost  

---

## 🎯 Objectives
The analysis was designed to answer key questions:

- Which countries are the most and least expensive for studying abroad?
- Which universities offer the most budget-friendly options?
- What is the total cost of studying (tuition + living + visa + insurance)?
- How does cost vary by program level (Bachelor’s, Master’s, PhD)?
- How does **duration influence total study cost?**

---

## 📂 Dataset Description
The dataset includes financial and academic attributes such as:

- Country & City  
- University & Program  
- Study Level (Bachelor’s, Master’s, PhD)  
- Duration  
- Tuition Fees  
- Living Costs  
- Rent  
- Visa Fees  
- Insurance  
- Exchange Rate  

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning & Transformation
Data preparation was performed using **Power Query**, including:

- Removed duplicates  
- Standardized column names and formats  
- Fixed structural inconsistencies  
- Created calculated columns:
  - Total Cost  
  - Cost per Year  
  - Adjusted Cost  
  - Duration (in years)  
  - Cost Categories (Low, Average, High)  

---

## 📊 Data Modeling & Measures
Using analytical logic and measures, the following KPIs were created:

- **Total Study Cost**
- **Average Study Cost**
- **Average Tuition**
- **Cost per Year**
- **Tuition Contribution (%)**
- **Average Cost per Country**
- **Cost Distribution by Level**

---

## 📈 Dashboard Design

This project is divided into **two dashboards** to enhance clarity and storytelling:

---

### 🌐 Dashboard 1: Global Cost Overview
**Purpose:** Provide a high-level view of study costs across countries  

#### Key Insights:
- USA and UK are the most expensive study destinations  
- Master’s programs have the highest overall cost  
- Certain universities dominate the high-cost range  
- **Cost fluctuates significantly with duration**  

---

### 💰 Dashboard 2: Affordability Analysis
**Purpose:** Focus on cost breakdown and budget-friendly options  

#### Key Insights:
- Tuition accounts for ~90% of total study cost  
- Majority of programs fall under the low-cost category  
- Cities like Bizerte, Aswan, and Gabes offer the lowest rent  
- Several universities provide affordable alternatives  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights & Findings

### 1. Tuition is the Primary Cost Driver
Tuition contributes the largest share of total cost, making it the most critical factor in decision-making.

---

### 2. Location Strongly Influences Cost
Countries and cities significantly impact overall expenses due to differences in living costs and rent.

---

### 3. Duration Does Not Follow a Linear Pattern ⚠️
One of the most important findings:

- Cost does not increase proportionally with duration  
- Some shorter programs are unexpectedly expensive  
- Some longer programs are relatively affordable  

#### 💡 Why this happens:
- Tuition structures vary by program and country  
- Fixed costs (visa, insurance) distort short-term costs  
- Exchange rates affect total cost inconsistently  

👉 This shows that **students should not assume longer duration = higher cost**

---

### 4. Affordable Options Exist
Despite high global costs, several universities and locations provide cost-effective alternatives.

## 💡 Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

- Students should evaluate **total cost, not just tuition**
- Consider **cost per year** for better comparison  
- Choose cities with lower living and rent costs  
- Analyze program duration carefully before deciding  
- Explore budget-friendly universities instead of focusing only on rankings
